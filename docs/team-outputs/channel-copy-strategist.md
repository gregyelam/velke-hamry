**Perspective: boutique short-term rental channel + copy strategist**

## 1. Channel Priority Matrix

| Channel | Est. % of bookings | Cost | Control | Who manages | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AirBnB | 55-65% | 3% host fee | High (direct listing) | Owners | P1 |
| Booking.com | 20-30% | 15-18% commission | Medium (via Muhu) | Muhu + owners | P2 |
| Own Instagram | Discovery only (0% direct) | Time only | Full | Owners | P2 (brand) |
| Muhu management page | Upstream influence on both OTAs | Implicit in commission | Low | Muhu | P3 (influence) |
| Own micro-site | 5-10% direct (year 2+) | 30-80 EUR/year + setup | Full | Owners | P3 (build later) |

**Key call:** AirBnB is the revenue engine and the easiest to control right now. Fix it first. Instagram builds the brand before the micro-site exists. Booking.com is on autopilot via Muhu - influence what you can, accept what you cannot.

---

### Posting cadence

1 post/week minimum. 2 posts/week realistic if owners batch photos in a session and schedule. Use Meta Business Suite or Later to schedule a week's worth in 30 min.

---

### Stories vs grid

Grid = permanent brand. Stories = ephemeral, lower stakes. Use Stories for:
- "Just snowed in Hamry" spontaneous shots
- Behind-the-scenes (cleaning day before guests, terasa in fog)
- Booking window reminders (once a month max)

Keep grid posts minimal-caption. Stories can be slightly warmer and casual.

---

### Reels: No (at this stage)

Reels need motion/editing effort. For a boutique 1BR with 2 owners managing on the side, Reels are a time sink with low ROI. Revisit if an account reaches 1,000+ followers and a clear rental season pattern emerges.

Keep it short. Direct to AirBnB link. Never quote prices in DM (AirBnB handles dynamic pricing). If a guest pushes for direct payment: only offer if you have a direct booking process set up (Stripe invoice + contract). Don't improvise.

## 5. Direct Channel (Own Micro-site)

### Worth building? Yes - but in year 2, not year 1.

Year 1 ROI: near-zero. Setup cost: 4-8 hours + 30-80 EUR/year (Squarespace/Cargo). Benefit: zero, because no organic traffic exists yet and Instagram is not yet driving discovery.

Year 2 logic: Once Instagram has 300+ followers, repeat guests, and word-of-mouth - a micro-site earns its keep as a trust signal and a direct booking lane.

---

### Recommended setup: 1-page Squarespace or Cargo at custom domain

Domain suggestion: `hamry.studio` or `apartmanhamry.cz` or `velkehamry.studio` (check availability). Not ".cabin" - that TLD has poor Czech SEO indexing.

---

### Content modules (in order)

1. Hero photo (full-bleed, terasa or fasada, no headline overlay - let the image lead)
2. One-sentence story: "Architektonicky apartman v Jizerskych horach. Navrzeno Studiem Reaktor."
3. Photo grid (6-8 images, click-to-expand)
4. Practical block: capacity (2), location (Velke Hamry), nearest town (Tanvald 7 min), Prague (87 min)
5. Calendar/availability: button "Zkontrolovat dostupnost" -> links to AirBnB
6. Instagram feed embed (auto-updates, keeps the page alive)
7. Minimal contact: email only, no phone

---

### Gateway only vs accept direct payments

**Recommendation: gateway only in year 1-2.** Direct booking via Stripe means: draft rental contract, handle cancellation policy, deposit collection, dispute resolution. That's 6-10 hours of setup and ongoing legal exposure. AirBnB guest guarantee and review system are worth the 3% fee while volumes are low.

Revisit direct bookings if occupancy hits 70%+ and repeat guest rate exceeds 20%.

## 6. Muhu Page Negotiation Talking Points

### 3 specific changes to request

1. **Apartment-specific photo section:** Ask Muhu to add a dedicated image gallery for your unit (not a mixed pool of all Muhu properties). This is reasonable - Booking.com supports per-property photos and most managers accommodate it.

2. **Update the property name:** "Hamry apartman - Studio Reaktor design" instead of whatever generic label currently appears. Muhu can do this via their Booking.com extranet without a process change.

3. **Rewrite the short property description:** Propose the 180-char text from Section 3 above. Frame it as improving conversion - lower OTA commission means more profit per booking for everyone.

---

### 3 non-negotiables (own regardless of what Muhu agrees to)

1. AirBnB listing - you own and control this directly. Never hand it to Muhu.
2. Instagram profile - owned by you. Muhu has no role here.
3. Guest review responses on AirBnB - always respond in your own voice.

---

### Fallback if Muhu refuses

If Muhu won't update the Booking.com listing: accept it as a secondary lead-gen channel with generic copy, and double down on AirBnB (full control) and Instagram (brand). Most design-led boutique rentals find that AirBnB guests convert better for this segment anyway - Booking.com skews budget/family. The Muhu page underperforming is not catastrophic.

## 7. Review Prompting Strategy

### Post-stay message template

Send 24 hours after checkout via AirBnB message thread.

**Czech:**

> Ahoj, doufame, ze jste si pobyt uzili. Kdybys mel/a chvilku, potesilo by nas recenze na Airbnb - pomaha dal hostem jako vy najit nas. A kdybys necel/a neco zlepsit, napiš nam rovnou - radi to uslyšime. Dekujeme a nashle v horach.

**English (for non-Czech guests):**

> Hi, hope you enjoyed the stay. If you have a moment, a review on Airbnb helps other guests find us - we'd really appreciate it. If anything wasn't quite right, let us know directly - we always want to improve. Thanks and hope to see you back in the mountains.

---

### Asking for specific feedback

Add a second paragraph to the Czech message if you want photo permission or referrals:

> Pokud by ses chtel/a podilit o fotky z pobytu, klidne nas oznac na Instagramu (@nazevuctu).

Do NOT ask for referrals explicitly - it reads as pushy. If guests love it, they'll tell friends unprompted.

---

### Handling 4-star or below

1. Do NOT respond defensively in the public review reply.
2. Public reply template: "Dekujeme za zpetnou vazbu. Tesite nas, ze jste dorazili. Uvedeny nedostatek bereme vazne a pracujeme na nem. Doufame, ze se uvidime znovu."
3. Privately message the guest to understand what went wrong - not to argue, to learn.
4. If the review is factually false: use AirBnB's dispute process (max 1 attempt, calm language).
5. One 4-star review does not matter. Five in a row on the same issue does.

## 8. Copy Voice Rules

### DO

1. Short sentences. One idea per sentence.
2. Sensory verbs: vonelo, zaskripalo, bylo slyšet, chlad rannich desek.
3. Weather words: mlha, mraz, deste, zlata hodina, cervankova zatazeno.
4. Specific place names: Velke Hamry, Tanvald, Jizery, Jizerske hory (not "mountains nearby").
5. Material names: borovice, tmave drevo, sageova zelen, lneny polstar.
6. Let silence stand - an image with no caption often outperforms one with four adjectives.
7. Use the Czech you actually speak. Avoid formal or marketing register.
8. Fragments are fine: "Rano. Ticho." is better than "Rano bylo ticho a klidne."
9. Anchor in time: "Leden." "Ranni mlha." "Po trekovani."
10. One CTA per piece of content at most - and only when needed.

### DON'T

1. Never use: luxury, premium, stunning, perfect, unique, breathtaking.
2. Never use exclamation marks in listing copy or captions.
3. Never write "ideal for couples" or "perfect romantic getaway" - let the guest infer.
4. Never explain the architecture at length - name it once, move on.
5. Never write pricing in captions or posts (AirBnB handles it).
6. Never use superlatives: "nejlepsi pohled", "neuvěritelny interier".
7. Never start a caption with "We are excited to share".
8. Never describe what a photo shows - caption adds what the photo cannot say.
9. Never use emoji in listing copy (one small exception: Instagram Stories only).
10. Never use en-dash or em-dash. Plain hyphen (-) everywhere.
