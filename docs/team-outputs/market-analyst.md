## 2. Competitive Landscape

**Named comparables to study:**

**Stara posta Krkonose** (Horni Mala Upa area)
- Strong: editorial-quality photography, fireplace, story-led copywriting, direct booking dominant
- Weak: older interior, limited summer appeal
- ADR: ~2,800-3,600 CZK weekend peak

**Chalupa Trae** (@cabin_trae, South Bohemia)
- The template to follow. Photo-led, Danish-minimal aesthetic, Instagram-first discovery
- Copywriting: "Inspirovana Severem, postavena na Jihu" - location story embedded in identity
- ADR: ~2,500-3,200 CZK. Direct bookings reportedly 30-40% of volume
- What they do right: seasonal mood shots, no stock photography, personal hosting voice

**Tiny House Jizerske hory** (search Airbnb for "design Jablonec nad Nisou" / "designovy apartman Jizerky")
- Small-footprint design units with terrace or forest position are gaining traction since 2022
- ADR: 1,600-2,400 CZK, typically under-priced relative to quality
- Opportunity: this asset outclasses most at photos + architecture level

**Penzion Jested area** (Liberec surroundings)
- Mass-market competition, Booking.com primary, race-to-bottom pricing
- Not a direct competitor once the design segment is established

**Mlynske udoli / boutique chalets near Harrachov**
- Closest geographic competitor class - 4-6 guest chalets, 3,000-5,000 CZK/night
- Families, not couples - different buyer

**Takeaway:** The design-couple segment (Prague, Brno, DE, PL origin) is underserved in Jizerske hory specifically. Krkonose has more supply. This is a positioning window.

## 3. Demand Drivers - Design-Conscious 28-45 Segment

**What they actually want:**
- A space that looks better in their photos than their apartment does. This is non-negotiable.
- Disconnection from routine - the "escape" narrative must be felt, not stated
- 87 min from Prague is ideal: close enough for impulse, far enough to feel like travel
- Architecture with a story they can retell ("forged ingots", Studio Reaktor - this is usable)

**Top 5 booking decision factors (ranked):**

1. **Photography** - single biggest lever. 3 aspirational hero shots close the deal or kill it. Current AirBnB photos are the #1 problem.
2. **Location story** - "Jizerky edge, iron forge heritage, dark-wood ingots" beats "mountain chalet near ski slopes"
3. **Reviews tone** - design-conscious guests read for voice, not just stars. One review saying "felt like a magazine shoot" is worth 20 clean-stay reviews
4. **Kitchen quality** - couples cooking together is part of the ritual. Induction hob, wine glasses, cast iron pan visible in photos
5. **Wifi + workspace** - remote workers are a weekday anchor. 100Mbps + desk or terrace table is a booking multiplier Mon-Thu

**Deal-killers:**
- Parking not confirmed upfront (this segment drives from Prague - confirm 1-2 spots in listing)
- Photos showing clutter, bad lighting, or worn furniture
- Generic or AI-sounding listing copy
- No check-in flexibility (late self-check-in is expected)
- Bathroom that looks budget (the one upgrade with highest photo ROI)

| Nov | Low | 1,300 | 1,800 | 1 night OK |

**Notes:**
- Christmas/NYE/Easter: surge to 3,800-4,500 CZK/night, 3-night min
- Remote-worker weekly rate: 9,500-11,000 CZK/week (Mon-Fri), nudge via direct channel
- Do not list below 1,300 CZK - floor protects brand positioning

**Annual occupancy target:** 62-68% (realistic for design segment with strong photography + Muhu channel reach). 55% is floor if photo/copy problem not fixed.

**Annual revenue projection:**
- 365 nights x 65% = 237 bookings nights
- Blended ADR across seasons: ~2,100 CZK
- Gross: ~498,000 CZK/year (~EUR 20,000)
- After Muhu fee (typically 20-25%): ~375,000-400,000 CZK net (~EUR 15,000-16,000)
- Upside at 70% occupancy + brand premium: ~460,000 CZK net

## 7. Risks Specific to This Asset

1. **Muhu generic brand dilution** - Muhu positions as volume operator. Their copy flattens the design story. If they control listing copy and photos, the premium is lost. Negotiate control over main photo order and listing title.

2. **Jizerky oversupply in family segment bleeding into design pricing** - Low-quality competitors discounting in shoulder season create a price anchor problem. Counter: hold floor, never discount below 1,300 CZK, use minimum stays to block low-value nights.

3. **Photography gap killing conversion** - Old photos with poor styling are actively suppressing CTR on Airbnb. A single re-shoot (3h, natural light, styled) is the highest-ROI action available. Risk if delayed: Airbnb algorithm deprioritises low-CTR listings.

4. **No direct booking infrastructure** - 100% dependence on Muhu/Airbnb means zero brand equity capture and no repeat-guest path. If Muhu is sold or changes terms, there is no fallback. Risk horizon: 2028 sale prep requires a trackable guest history.

5. **Co-ownership coordination friction (Smeky)** - Personal use conflicts, pricing disagreements, and aesthetic divergence (FJORD HAMRY vs TRÆ approach) can stall execution. Agree on: photo budget, listing copy ownership, minimum price floor, and personal-use calendar by end of June 2026.
